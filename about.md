    <h2>My Story</h2>
    <p>Hi, I'm Vishal. Like most people in this space, my journey started with wanting to build some fancy chatbot or a model. But underneath that, I always had this curiosity about how things actually worked under the hood.</p>

    <p>So I went deep into data science and machine learning — and deep here mostly means maths. I've spent the better part of five years studying mathematics across different contexts — competitive exams, entrance prep, and now machine learning. It's been my favourite subject since high school, and for good reason: maths is the universal language. Its principles don't change whether you're working at the quantum scale or across celestial distances. That kind of consistency is rare. And it's exactly why I believe the goal should never be to memorize — it should be to build a perspective of your own.</p>

    <p>The deeper I went into ML, the more I realized how many foundational topics get rushed through — degrees of freedom, the gamma function, why we divide by n-1, what a chi-squared distribution really is, how most probability distributions are connected to each other. So I decided to go back and actually understand them properly.</p>
  </div>

  <div class="about-section">
    <h2>What This Site Is</h2>
    <p>This is where I document that process. My notes, breakdowns, and findings on the fundamental topics of data science and machine learning — starting from intuition and building toward the math, not the other way around.</p>

    <p>I'm building this through content — writeups, YouTube videos, short-form reels, whatever format works best for the topic. The platform doesn't matter. The only thing that matters is making you see what mathematics really is, from the subject's own perspective, so you can build your own.</p>

    <p>If you're someone who struggled with math because it was taught incomplete, not because it was hard — you've found the right place.</p>
